# The DanceSoul Method™

The proprietary practice at the core of DanceSoulTherapy — and the asset that makes retreats, certification, an academy, and a franchise possible. This document is the IP. Treat it as confidential.

> Positioning: an emotional-wellbeing practice, not medical treatment. See §7 (Scope & Safety).

---

## 1. Premise

Emotion lives in the body. Stress, grief, and tension we don't process are held as physical patterns — a tight chest, a guarded posture, a held breath. Talking reaches the mind; it rarely reaches the body. **The DanceSoul Method uses guided movement and breath to let the body release what it holds** — safely, without choreography, without performance.

One line: *Meditation stills the mind. The DanceSoul Method moves the body to free it.*

---

## 2. The five principles (the “how”)

These are non-negotiable and define the Method. A facilitator who breaks them isn't practising it.

1. **No choreography.** Movement is free and intuitive. There is nothing to get right.
2. **Breath leads.** Breath is the thread; movement follows it, not the music alone.
3. **Safety before expression.** The container (trust, no judgement, opt-out) is built before any release is invited.
4. **Follow the body, not the mind.** Sensation and impulse guide the session, not a plan.
5. **The facilitator holds, doesn't perform.** The facilitator creates the space; the participant is the practice.

---

## 3. The Arc — session structure

Every session, private or group, follows the same five-phase arc. This repeatable structure is what makes the experience consistent across facilitators and locations — the basis of certification and franchise. The middle three phases embody the brand signature *Awaken · Express · Heal*.

| Phase | Intent | Body | Breath | Music |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 · **Arrive** | Land, settle, consent | Stillness, gentle sway | Slow, deepening | Ambient, sparse |
| 2 · **Awaken** | Wake the body, build trust | Mobilise, open joints | Sync breath to motion | Warm, rising tempo |
| 3 · **Express** | Free release — the peak | Full free movement | Dynamic, expressive | Driving, emotional |
| 4 · **Release** | Surrender, let go | Slowing, floor, softening | Long exhales | Descending, tender |
| 5 · **Integrate** | Return, reflect, carry out | Stillness, self-touch | Natural, quiet | Silence → soft |

**The tempo map** (music rising then falling across the five phases) is proprietary and is taught as a curated, licence-controlled playlist system.

## 4. Facilitation protocol

- **Space.** Low light, warm; clear floor; no mirrors (removes performance/judgement). Water available.
- **Opening contract.** State the frame in 60 seconds: no level, no judgement, opt out of anything, eyes closed welcome. This builds the container (Principle 3).
- **Cueing language.** Invitational, never directive — “allow,” “let,” “notice,” never “do this.” A defined vocabulary is part of the curriculum.
- **Emotional safety.** Movement can surface emotion; that is expected and welcome. Facilitator stays present, non-intrusive, never interprets. Clear opt-out and grounding technique for overwhelm.
- **Closing.** Always land in stillness + integration before re-entry. Never end at the peak.

## 7. Scope & safety (guardrails)

- Positioned as an **emotional-wellbeing and movement practice**, not psychotherapy or medical treatment. No diagnosis, no cure claims.
- Facilitators work **within scope**: hold space, do not counsel or interpret.
- **Referral protocol:** if a participant needs clinical support, refer to a licensed professional; defined in training.
- Standard wellness waiver + medical self-screening at intake.
- This governs all copy (see Blueprint claim-governance) and all facilitator conduct.
